Introduction to the NES Controller

Created in 1978, it took the world by storm becoming the most widely-known controller in Nintendo History. Using a simple design with a d-pad and four buttons it helped the controller become widely successful with the public. It's use with the incredibly popular Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) brought it to become easily recognizable with pop culture and in the gaming community as a whole.

The Purpose

The main purpose of the NES controller is to provide a simple and easy to learn controller for the NES platform. With just a d-pad and four other buttons it is simple for newcomers to gaming when household videos games became mainstream.

The Design

The layout of the NES Controller is iconic in the gaming community. With the D-pad on the left side, start and select button in the middle, and two round buttons on the right it became a simple and easy to learn controller.
The wire used to connect to the main console is a proprietary piece of hardware. It uses a 4021 IC that transmits what buttons have been pushed.

User Guide

Getting Started

  1. Unbox the NES controller set, ensuring both controllers are present.
  2. Charge the controllers by attaching them to a Nintendo Switch or Nintendo Switch â€“ OLED Model console.
  3. Ensure the console is powered on and ready for gameplay.

Connecting the Controller

  1. Attach the controller to the console via the charging rail for charging.
  2. Once charged, detach the controller; it will connect wirelessly to the console.
  3. Confirm the controller is recognized in the console's controller settings.

Basic Controls

  • D-Pad: Navigate the game selection menu or move within a game.
  • A Button (Left Controller): Take a screenshot.
  • Hold A Button (Left Controller): Take a video capture.
  • B Button (Left Controller): Open the Suspend Menu.
  • Right D-Pad: Access the HOME Menu.

Using with Nintendo Switch Lite

  • L Button: Take a screenshot.
  • Hold L Button: Take a video capture.
  • R Button: Return to the HOME Menu.
  • Hold R Button: Open Quick Settings Menu.
  • L + R Buttons: Open Suspend Menu or quit online play (only while in a game joined as a guest).

Advanced Controls

  • Turbo Mode: Hold the B button and quickly tap the D-Pad for rapid movement in supported games.
  • Custom Button Mapping: Adjust the controller settings on your console to remap buttons according to your preferences.
  • Dual Controller Usage: Certain games allow simultaneous use of both controllers for two-player modes or advanced control schemes.

Maintenance Tips

  • Regularly clean the controller with a dry cloth; avoid using water or cleaning agents that may damage electronics.
  • Store controllers in a dry, cool place to prevent wear on the buttons and cord.
  • Ensure the charging port is free of dust and debris for proper charging.

Game-Specific Tips

  • In platformer games, hold the B button to run faster and jump with more control.
  • For puzzle games, use the D-Pad precisely to navigate menus and position pieces accurately.
  • In multiplayer games, coordinate button usage between controllers for smooth gameplay.

Safety Guidelines

  • Avoid excessive force on buttons or the D-Pad to prevent mechanical damage.
  • Do not use the controller near liquids or in extreme temperatures.
  • Take breaks during long gaming sessions to avoid fatigue or discomfort.

Tips for Best Use

  • Keep the controller within range of the console to avoid disconnection.
  • Hold the B button to run faster in games that support it.
  • Use the D-Pad carefully to avoid moving in the wrong direction during gameplay.
  • Regularly check battery level to ensure uninterrupted gaming sessions.

Troubleshooting

If your controller is not responding or is responding incorrectly to your input, the following information will help diagnose the issue.

Official Troubleshooting Guidelines

Step 1: Ensure proper connection

Controller should be plugged into Port No. 1 on your system.

Step 2: Disconnect your device

Power off your system and disconnect your controller by pressing the tabs on the connector that goes into the console

Step 3: Check for physical damage

Check for any visible damage on your controller and along the cord.
If any physical damage is found, controller should be replaced. If no damage is present, continue to step 4.

Step 4: Reconnect controller

Reconnect your device into Port No. 1. If your device is still not working, continue to step 5.

Step 5: Check another controller

If you have another compatible controller, plug that device into Port No. 1. If the new controller is also faulty, this is indicative of an issue with the system.
